Projects

Below is a collection of both my personal projects and others that I've contributed to.

Cornell Autonomous Sailboat Team

Navigation Team Lead

The Cornell Autonomous Sailboat Team, or CUSail, aims to build a completely autonomous robotic sailboat for competition at the Sailbot International Robotic Sailing Regatta. As the lead of the Navigation subteam, I lead a team of undergraduates to develop the software and hardware that allowed the boat to sail to a target location. More information about CUSail can be found on their website.

Piano Playing Robot

Personal Project

This was a project I undertook the summer after my sophomore year at Cornell. I had been trying to teach myself to play piano, but I just wasn't getting it, so I decided to build a robot to play for me. If you're interested in seeing how I pieced it together, you can check out my page on the Arduino Project Hub. Forgive the mess, version 2.0 is the in the works and promises to be a big improvement.

Maze Mapping Robot

Cornell ECE 3400 Project

During my junior year at Cornell, I worked with a team to build a maze mapping robot for a class project. It uses depth first search to visit every block in the maze and build an occupancy map. It also uses IR sensors to detect other robots in the maze and avoid collisions.
